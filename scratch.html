Why Golf?

Golf is more than a sport — it's a pursuit of mastery. Every round presents a new challenge: wind, elevation, course layout, and your own mental fortitude. I fell in love with golf for the same reason many do: the rare, fleeting feeling of a perfectly struck iron.

Whether it's an early morning tee time with mist still on the fairway or a competitive round with friends, golf teaches lessons that extend far beyond the course.

The Four Majors

The pinnacle of professional golf — four prestigious tournaments that define a player's legacy.

  1. The Masters Tournament – Augusta National, Georgia
    • Held every April
    • Winner receives the iconic Green Jacket
    • Known for Amen Corner (holes 11–13)
  2. The U.S. Open – Rotating U.S. venues
    • Renowned for grueling course setups
    • Emphasizes accuracy over power
    • Held in mid-June
  3. The Open Championship – Links courses, UK
    • The oldest Major, dating back to 1860
    • Wind and weather are major factors
    • Winner receives the Claret Jug
  4. The PGA Championship – Rotating U.S. venues
    • Hosted by the PGA of America
    • Features the strongest field of any Major
    • Known as "Glory's Last Shot" in August
